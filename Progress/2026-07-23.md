# Day 001 - Talking About Breakfast

**Date:** 2026-07-23

---

# Topic

Today I practiced talking about my breakfast and eating habits. Instead of giving only short answers, I explained my opinions, habits, and reasons in English.

---

# Conversation Summary

We talked about:

* My daily breakfast routine.
* Why I don't eat the same breakfast every day.
* My favorite McDonald's breakfast.
* Why I don't like eating too much meat in the morning.
* Why I would choose a breakfast buffet if it were free.
* Why I usually start with a sandwich.

For the first time, I was able to answer several follow-up questions without switching to Chinese.

---

# Biggest Improvement

Today, I didn't stop because I didn't know what to say.

Instead, I tried to explain my ideas by using **because**, giving examples, and adding more details.

I also began expressing my personal opinions instead of only describing facts.

---

# Biggest Challenge

I still need to improve:

* Articles (a / an / the)
* Singular and plural nouns
* Natural expressions
* Verb patterns (such as **like + V-ing**)
* Word order

These mistakes did not stop the conversation, but making them more natural will be my next goal.

---

# Today's English Discoveries

### Vocabulary

* **jam** → fruit spread for bread or toast
* **sauce** → liquid sauce used for cooking or pouring
* **spread butter**
* **soy milk**
* **protein**
* **ingredients**
* **different kinds of...**

### Useful Expressions

* I don't even...
* It only takes...
* It gives me a good start to the day.
* I usually start with...
* Another reason is that...
* I don't like eating...
* better for me

---

# Reflection

Today was an important lesson.

At first, I thought my biggest problem was vocabulary.

However, I realized that my real challenge is learning how to continue a conversation naturally.

When I explained **why** I liked something, the conversation became much easier.

I also noticed that I don't need perfect English to communicate. As long as I keep talking, I can improve step by step.

---

# Next Goal

For the next conversation, I want to:

* Ask at least one question back.
* Give longer answers naturally.
* Use **because** and **another reason is...** more often.
* Speak with more confidence instead of worrying about mistakes.
